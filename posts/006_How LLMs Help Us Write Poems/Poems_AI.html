AI as a Creative Assistant

How Large Language Models Help Us Write Poems

COMM4190 Blog Post

Claim: LLMs make strong muses—not replacements. They accelerate metaphor-finding, style-shifting, and revision loops while humans steer feeling, taste, and truth.

Why Poetry Loves Tools (and Resists Automation)

  • Compression: Big feelings in tiny spaces.
  • Music: Stress patterns, line breaks, sonic texture.
  • Surprise: The mind-turn that cracks a window open.

Tension: LLMs can simulate surface music, but the pulse of necessity—the reason this line must exist—remains human work.

# Pseudo-code: Brainstorm 12 fresh metaphors for "loneliness".
prompt = """
Give me 12 non-cliché metaphors for 'loneliness'.
Rules:
- Concrete images, no 'void', no 'empty room'.
- Prefer tactile or audible scenes.
- Each under 12 words.
"""
metaphors = LLM.generate(prompt)
print(metaphors)

Output: Metaphor Orchard

  • A kettle singing to a kitchen with no chairs.
  • Footsteps rehearsing for guests who do not arrive.
  • A porch swing counting the evening without you.
  • A lighthouse blinking to ships that changed their route.
  • A violin tuned to a concert the hall forgot.
  • A seed packet waiting past the rain.
# Pseudo-code: Form play—request multiple versions of a theme.
prompt = """
Write three mini-poems (<= 5 lines each) about the sea and memory:
1) a haiku,
2) free verse,
3) a Shakespearean couplet.
Avoid clichés; use fresh imagery.
"""
mini_poems = LLM.generate(prompt)
print(mini_poems)

Co-Writing Protocol

  1. Spark: Ask for 10 openings; pick 1.
  2. Expand: Request 3 continuations in different tones.
  3. Weave: Stitch favorite lines; remove scaffolding.
  4. Listen: Read aloud; cut until it breathes.
  5. Glaze: Subtle alliteration, consonance, or a motif (e.g., salt, brass, moth).

“Draft with abundance, revise with mercy.”

# Pseudo-code: Generate openings, then branch by tone.
prompt_open = "Give me 8 opening lines for a poem about time and salt."
opens = LLM.generate(prompt_open)

prompt_branch = f"""
Using this opening: "{'{opens[0]}' if 'opens' in globals() else 'Time keeps its pockets full of salt.'}",
continue for 6-8 lines in three tones:
- Lullaby
- Documentary
- Surreal
Label each version.
"""
branches = LLM.generate(prompt_branch)
print(branches)

Tone Shifts (Imagined Snippets)

  • Lullaby: “Sleep, little harbor; the tide folds your name like linen.”
  • Documentary: “At 0400 the salinity rose; we logged it, hands stinging.”
  • Surreal: “Clocks dissolved into brine; tongues learned the alphabet of foam.”

Takeaway: LLMs are excellent at rapid tone-hopping → perfect for exploration.

# Pseudo-code: Constraint engine—syllables and rhyme prompts (assist, not enforce).
line = "The moon keeps ledgers of uncounted debts."
prompt = f"""
Rewrite this line in iambic pentameter (≈10 syllables, unstress/stress pattern).
Offer 4 alternatives; optional slant rhyme with 'nets' or 'steps'.
Line: {line}
"""
variants = LLM.generate(prompt)
print(variants)

Editing: From Plenty to Precision

  • Cull ornaments: Remove lines that merely restate the image.
  • Sharpen verbs: Prefer “scalds” over “is hot,” “hoards” over “has.”
  • Test breath: Read aloud; a stumble is a cut mark.
  • Meaning pass: Ask: What changed from first to last line?

Micro-ritual: Replace one noun with a material (brass, linen, loam) and one verb with a craft verb (braze, card, leach).

# Pseudo-code: Human-in-the-loop polishing
raw_poem = """
The kettle hums to nobody.
Salt creeps into the windowsill.
I write your name in steam
and watch the alphabet dry.
"""
prompt = f"""
Edit this poem lightly:
- Keep the core images.
- Improve music (assonance/consonance).
- Tighten the last line with a turn.
Text: {raw_poem}
"""
edit = LLM.generate(prompt)
print(edit)

Coda: The Muse That Returns the Question

A model can hand you a thousand doors;
only you know which room you mean.

It offers echoes—salt, brass, moth—but you choose what sings.
Where the machine proposes, the poet disposes.
Where the model finishes a line, the human begins a life.
